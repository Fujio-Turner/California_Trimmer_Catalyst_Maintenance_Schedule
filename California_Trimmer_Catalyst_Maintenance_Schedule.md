### Quick Steps for Lubrication (Bottom-Line How-To)

1. **Prep**: Park on level ground, engine off and cool, remove [spark plug](https://grokipedia.com/page/Spark_plug) wire for safety. Clean off dirt around fittings/areas first (dry brush or cloth—no high-pressure water, as it washes away lube and causes corrosion).

2. **Grease Main Drive Shaft Bearings** (every 20 hrs):
   - Locate the 2 [zerk](https://grokipedia.com/page/Grease_fitting)/grease fittings on each side of the main drive shaft (under top cover—may need to tilt or remove cover partially).
   - Attach [grease gun](https://grokipedia.com/page/Grease_gun_(tool)) to fitting.
   - Pump slowly (2-4 pumps usually enough) until grease starts to emerge or you feel back-pressure.
   - Wipe excess grease.

3. **Lube Drum Roller Drive Chain** (every 40 hrs):
   - Remove/open side cover to access chain.
   - Shake chain lube can, spray evenly along chain while slowly rotating drum/chain (or apply with brush if not spray).
   - Let penetrate 5-10 min, wipe off excess to avoid attracting dirt.

4. **Light Lube for Cables/Levers/Etc.** (as needed):
   - Spray or drop light oil on pivot points, cable ends, height adjuster threads/knobs.
   - Work levers/cables several times to distribute.
   - Avoid over-spraying—excess attracts dust.

5. **Engine Oil Check/Change**:
   - Check level before each use ([dipstick](https://grokipedia.com/page/Dipstick)/filler cap).
   - Change per Honda manual (typically after first 5-8 hrs, then every 50 hrs or annually).
   - Drain old oil, refill with 10W-30 to full mark.

Follow the manual's warnings: Most other [bearings](https://grokipedia.com/page/Ball_bearing) are sealed (no grease needed). Store indoors if possible, and always double-check the separate Honda engine manual for oil specifics. If you mow heavily or in wet/dusty conditions, do these more frequently.

This keeps your Catalyst running smooth and extends life—let me know if you need photos or more details on any step!
### Belt Replacement

The Catalyst uses three drive belts. Inspect belts periodically for cracking, fraying, or glazing and replace as needed. Adjust cable tension when levers reach 1/2 travel distance to the handles — this indicates belt stretch.

| Belt | Type | Manual Part # | Parts List Part # | Replacement How-To |
|------|------|--------------|-------------------|-------------------|
| Engine drive | [Cogged](https://grokipedia.com/page/Toothed_belt) | C0519 | 1050450 | Remove top cover → remove top [idler](https://grokipedia.com/page/Idler-Loss_and_timing) → slide belt off larger front cogged [pulley](https://grokipedia.com/page/Pulley) → remove from engine pulley |
| Cartridge drive | [V-type](https://grokipedia.com/page/V-belt) | C0501 | 1050665 | Remove side cover → remove outer idler bracket → remove belt from pulleys |
| Rear roller drive | [V-type](https://grokipedia.com/page/V-belt) | C0526 | 1050806 | Remove side cover → remove outer idler bracket → remove belt from pulleys |

> **Tip:** Order spare belts to have on hand — they tend to break at the worst times!

#### ✂️ PRO TIP: Backlapping — Keep Your Reel Sharp Between Grinds

[Backlapping](https://grokipedia.com/page/Backlapping) is the process of spinning the reel in reverse against the bedknife with a lapping compound to restore a sharp, clean-cutting edge — like honing a knife. Do this **every 20–40 hours** or whenever you notice the grass tearing instead of slicing cleanly.

#### 🧊 PRO TIP: Off-Season / Winter Storage Checklist

Don't just park it in the garage and forget about it. Proper winter storage prevents corrosion, gummed carburetors, and stale fuel damage.

1. **Fuel** — Run the engine dry, or add [fuel stabilizer](https://www.amazon.com/s?k=Sta-Bil+fuel+stabilizer) (e.g., Sta-Bil) and run for 2 min to circulate
2. **Oil** — Change engine oil while warm (contaminants drain out easier)
3. **Reel & Bedknife** — Clean thoroughly, backlap if needed, then apply a light coat of oil (e.g., WD-40 or 3-in-1) to prevent rust
4. **Air Filter** — Clean or replace (Honda GX160 filter is cheap: [Amazon](https://www.amazon.com/s?k=Honda+GX160+air+filter) · [AutoZone](https://www.autozone.com/search?searchText=Honda+GX160+air+filter))
5. **Spark Plug** — Inspect and replace if worn ([Amazon](https://www.amazon.com/s?k=Honda+GX160+spark+plug+NGK) · [AutoZone](https://www.autozone.com/search?searchText=Honda+GX160+spark+plug))
6. **Grease** — Hit all zerk fittings before storage
7. **Belts** — Inspect for cracks/wear; replace if questionable so you're ready for spring
8. **Storage** — Store indoors in a dry area; cover if in a shed or garage with moisture

> **Spring startup:** Check oil, fresh fuel, test the paper cut — and you're good to go!
